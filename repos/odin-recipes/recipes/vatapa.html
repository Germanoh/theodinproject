Vatapa

Description

Brazilian seafood stew served in almost any city on Brazil, on São João festival is served typically
in a small plate accompanied with paçoca (shreded sun dried meat) and rice. It takes lots of pepper also.

Ingredients

  • 2 chopped onions
  • ½ a cup of dried shrimp (see notes)
  • 2 to 3 cloves of chopped garlic
  • 1 to 3 malagueta or jalapeño chile peppers, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 and ½ cups of stock or water
  • ½ a cup of natural peanut or cashew butter
  • 1 cup of breadcrumbs
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 cups of coconut milk
  • ¼ of dendê, or red palm oil (optional)

Steps

  1. Step 1
    Place the onion, dried shrimp, garlic and chilies in a food processor or blender and puree well. Add a little water if
    necessary.

  2. Step 2
    Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion-shrimp mixture and saute until cooked through,
    about 5 to 7 minutes.

  3. Step 3
    Stir in the stock or water and whisk in the peanut or cashew butter until smooth. Then stir in the breadcrumbs, salt and
    pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 5 to 8 minutes to meld the flavors.

  4. Step 4
    Stir in the shrimp and coconut milk and simmer another 5 or 6 minutes, or until shrimp is almost cooked through.

  5. Step 5
    Remove from heat, stir in the dendê oil and serve over rice or alongside acarajé bean fritters.
